Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how Awantrix (“Awantrix”, “we”, “us”, or “our”) collects, uses, discloses, and safeguards information in connection with our websites, services, and communications.

By accessing or using our website or services, you agree to the terms of this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please discontinue use of our website and services.

Last updated: December 24, 2025

1. Introduction

Awantrix provides connectivity advisory and project coordination services, including support with Spectrum business internet, Verizon, Frontier, Comcast, and other carriers. In delivering these services, we may collect and process certain information about visitors to our website, prospective customers, and existing customers and partners.

This Privacy Policy is intended to give you a clear understanding of what information we collect, how we use it, with whom we share it, and the choices you have regarding your information. We seek to collect and use only the information that is reasonably necessary to provide and improve our services, operate our business, and meet our legal and contractual obligations.

This Policy applies to information we collect through:

  • Our website and landing pages;
  • Contact and quote request forms;
  • Email and other direct communications with you; and
  • Any other online or offline channels that link to or reference this Policy.

2. Information We Collect

We collect information that you provide directly to us, information that we automatically collect when you use our website, and information that we may obtain from third parties in the course of providing our services.

2.1 Information You Provide to Us

When you interact with us, you may choose to provide the following types of information:

  • Contact information: such as your name, job title, company name, email address, phone number, and mailing address.
  • Business and project details: including service locations, connectivity requirements, bandwidth needs, timelines, and other information relevant to quotes or engagements.
  • Account and communication preferences: such as your preferences regarding how we contact you and what information you wish to receive.
  • Support and correspondence: information included in messages you send to us, responses to surveys, or feedback about our services.

2.2 Information Collected Automatically

When you visit our website, we may automatically collect certain information about your device and how you interact with our site. This may include:

  • Usage data: pages visited, links clicked, referring/exit pages, timestamps, and other interactions with our site.
  • Device and technical data: IP address, browser type, operating system, device identifiers, and similar technical information.
  • Approximate location data: inferred from your IP address, to help us understand where our website is being accessed.

This information is typically collected using cookies and similar tracking technologies. See the “Cookies and Similar Technologies” section below for more detail.

2.3 Information from Third Parties

In certain cases, we may receive information about you from third parties, such as carriers, channel partners, data enrichment providers, or public sources, in order to validate business contact details, understand service availability, or support project work. We use this information in accordance with this Privacy Policy and any applicable agreements or laws.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for legitimate business purposes, including to provide and improve our services, communicate with you, and protect our legal rights. Specifically, we may use your information to:

  • Respond to inquiries, quote requests, and other communications you submit;
  • Design, source, and coordinate connectivity solutions with carriers and partners;
  • Operate, maintain, and improve our website, content, and user experience;
  • Personalize content or communications based on your interests or interactions;
  • Send administrative messages, updates, technical notices, and support information;
  • Send marketing or promotional communications where permitted by law and your preferences;
  • Analyze usage trends and performance for security, analytics, and optimization;
  • Detect, prevent, and respond to fraud, abuse, security incidents, and other harmful activity; and
  • Comply with applicable laws, regulations, legal processes, and enforce our agreements.

3.1 Legal Bases for Processing (Where Applicable)

Where required by applicable law (such as in the European Economic Area or United Kingdom), we process personal information only when we have a valid legal basis, including: (i) your consent; (ii) performance of a contract or to take steps at your request prior to entering a contract; (iii) our legitimate interests (for example, to operate and improve our services, communicate with you, or ensure security); or (iv) compliance with a legal obligation.

4. Cookies and Similar Technologies

We may use cookies, pixel tags, scripts, and similar technologies (collectively, “cookies”) to operate our website, understand usage, and improve performance. Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device when you visit a website.

4.1 Types of Cookies We May Use

  • Strictly necessary cookies: required for the website to function, such as enabling basic navigation and security features.
  • Performance and analytics cookies: help us understand how visitors use our website so we can measure and improve performance.
  • Functional cookies: allow the site to remember choices you make (such as language or region) and provide enhanced features.
  • Marketing or advertising cookies: may be used, where implemented, to deliver or measure advertising and understand the effectiveness of campaigns.

4.2 Your Cookie Choices

Most web browsers automatically accept cookies by default, but you can modify your browser settings to decline cookies or alert you when cookies are being placed on your device. If you choose to disable cookies, some features of our website may not function properly.

Where required by law, we will request your consent for the use of non‑essential cookies. You may withdraw or modify your consent at any time by adjusting your browser settings or applicable consent tools, where available.

5. Sharing and Retention of Information

5.1 How We Share Information

We do not sell your personal information. We may share information in the following circumstances:

  • Service providers: with third‑party vendors who perform services on our behalf, such as hosting, analytics, communications, or form processing.
  • Carriers and partners: with internet service providers (e.g., Spectrum, Verizon, Frontier, Comcast, and others) and channel partners as necessary to obtain quotes, design solutions, or coordinate installations at your request.
  • Business transfers: in connection with a merger, acquisition, financing, or sale of all or a portion of our business, subject to appropriate confidentiality protections.
  • Legal and safety: when we believe disclosure is necessary to comply with a legal obligation, protect our rights, investigate fraud or security issues, or protect the safety of our users or the public.

5.2 Data Retention

We retain personal information for as long as reasonably necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this Policy, including to provide our services, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements. The specific retention period may vary depending on the nature of the information and our legal or contractual requirements. When information is no longer needed, we will take reasonable steps to delete, anonymize, or aggregate it.

6. Your Rights and Choices

Depending on your location and applicable law, you may have certain rights in relation to your personal information, including:

  • The right to request access to the personal information we hold about you;
  • The right to request correction or update of inaccurate or incomplete information;
  • The right to request deletion of your personal information, subject to legal or contractual obligations;
  • The right to object to or restrict certain types of processing;
  • The right to withdraw consent where we rely on your consent for processing; and
  • The right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority in your jurisdiction.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details in the “Contact Us” section below. We may need to verify your identity before responding to your request and may not be able to fulfill a request where we are required to retain certain data for legal, accounting, or other legitimate reasons.

You may also opt out of marketing emails at any time by following the unsubscribe instructions included in such emails or by contacting us directly. Please note that even if you opt out of marketing communications, we may still send you non‑marketing messages relating to your use of our services.

7. Data Security and International Transfers

We implement reasonable technical and organizational measures designed to protect the information we process from unauthorized access, use, alteration, or disclosure. However, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

Awantrix may process and store information in countries other than the country where you are located, including the United States. These countries may have data protection laws that differ from those in your jurisdiction. Where required, we take steps to ensure that appropriate safeguards are in place for cross‑border transfers, consistent with applicable data protection laws.

8. Children’s Privacy

Our website and services are intended for business and professional use and are not directed to children under the age of 16 (or other age as defined by applicable law). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If we become aware that we have collected personal information from a child without appropriate consent, we will take steps to delete such information. If you believe we may have collected information from a child, please contact us using the details below.

9. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology, legal requirements, or other factors. When we make material changes, we will update the “Last updated” date at the top of this page and, where appropriate, provide additional notice (such as by posting a prominent notice on our website). We encourage you to review this Policy periodically to stay informed about how we handle your information.
